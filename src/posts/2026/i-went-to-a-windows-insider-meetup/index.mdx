I saw that there was going to be a Microsoft Windows Insider meetup in London. I
registered for it, thinking ‘why not’.

A while later, I got an invite to the event (probably due to someone else
cancelling). I was unsure if I actually wanted to go (is it really the kind of
thing I want to go to?), but I was free at the time of the event, so I confirmed
my attendance (now thinking ‘what’s the worst that could happen?’).

The event was a two-hour affair yesterday evening in London. I was given an Uber
voucher to help me get to the event. I’m sure some people were able to get some
use from the voucher, but it was of little practical use for me for getting into
central London.

The evening’s informal programme consisted of 30 minutes of mingling (with
various Microsoft people available to talk to), 30 minutes of presentations
(including demos of upcoming changes), and another hour of mingling (though I
left a few minutes before the end, so it might’ve lingered on a bit longer).

There were canapés, booze, and, after the presentations, a buffet (including
copious prawns), so it was a decent affair overall. (And the room was cool and
breezy despite the lack of air con, a pleasant relief after coming in from the
heat outside.)

Pretty generous goody bags were given out. They contained a Logitech MX Master 4
mouse, Satechi SM1 mechanical keyboard, a branded mouse mat and a mouse charging
mat. (Sadly, though, I’m not going to get much use from the keyboard as it has a
US layout. And, if I’m being perfectly honest, staring at something that has the
US spelling of ‘programme’[^1] on it every day would also annoy me no end.)

There was also a free raffle (i.e. a ballot) giving you a chance of winning a
Surface device, but my ticket number wasn’t one of the two drawn.

On a productive note, I managed to find someone to provide some File Explorer
~~complaints~~ feedback to. I decided to lead with pointing out how ‘Duplicate
tab’ doesn’t duplicate any state (like scroll position or navigation history).
Not preserving any state isn’t particularly helpful if you want to carry on with
what you were doing (and it makes it more of an ‘open a new tab in the same
folder’ than a ‘duplicate tab’). (My reference point here is Firefox and
Chrome[^2], which both happily preserve the navigation history and scroll
position when duplicating a tab.)

Whether my feedback will go anywhere, I don’t know (and, well, it’s just
feedback from one person, transcribed into a Notepad tab). If the ‘duplicate
tab’ behaviour does ever change in File Explorer, you can thank, or blame, me.

[^1]:
    The space bar on the keyboard is branded ‘Windows Insider Program’ (as is
    the mouse mat). I suppose I could Tippex on the extra ‘m’ and ‘e’…

[^2]:
    Firefox and Chrome are, of course, both web browsers rather than file
    managers, so you might want to be cautious when drawing comparisons or
    copying their behaviour exactly. But I think the general principle is the
    same – when you clone a tab, I (at least) want to be able to carry on
    working as I was (which is in the new tab initially, because it’s
    automatically activated).

I thought Microsoft had got a handle on buggy Windows updates. Alas, since
installing the KB5095093 preview update for Windows 11 25H2, the audio on my
desktop PC is occasionally broken after resuming from sleep.

Broken here means that applications get stuck when trying to play audio. Videos
on the web, for example, just hang in a weird way.

The problem has persisted with the non-preview KB5101650 July 2026 update
recently released, and also with the KB5121767 out-of-band update that followed
it.

Some reports of this problem have also cropped up on the Microsoft forums in the
last week:

- [Windows Update KB5100998 Audio Issues](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-gb/answers/questions/5948960/windows-update-kb5100998-audio-issues)
- [WIN11 - NO SOUND](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-gb/answers/questions/5951833/win11-no-sound)

There’s also
[a two-month-old report of this problem on the Feedback Hub](https://aka.ms/AA10yd3p).
(The earlier date might be explained by the problem first cropping up in an
insider preview version.)

As you can imagine, the problem is very annoying. And so, when the problem has
happened, I’ve been trying various things to fix things with the least amount of
faff. After a fair bit of fiddling, I’ve found two reliable ways to get audio
working again on my PC[^1] (without resorting to restarting my computer).

For the first method, I navigate to the **Sound** page in **Settings** and click
on my output device:

<CaptionedImage
  src={settings_sound_webp}
  alt="The Sound page in the Windows Settings app"
  caption="You can get to this page by right-clicking on the sound icon in the system tray"
/>

Then, I change **Audio enhancements** to another option, and then change it
back:

<CaptionedImage
  src={settings_output_webp}
  alt="The properties page for an audio output in the Windows Settings app"
  caption="Of course, it’s very non-specific about what exactly these audio enhancements are"
/>

(In my case, I toggle it from **Off** to **Device Default Effects** and then
back.)

The second method of fixing things involves using the
[foobar2000](https://www.foobar2000.org) audio player. Simply temporarily
playing something using the relevant exclusive output device option (e.g.
‘Default : Primary Sound Driver [exclusive]’) makes things work again. (You can
switch the output device back afterwards.)

Unfortunately for me, I ran Disk Clean-up after installing KB5095093 and no
longer have the option of simply uninstalling the relevant updates. (That also
makes it difficult for me to conclusively prove that the update is what
triggered the problem, though it was the most recent thing that had changed when
the problem started.)

Giving Microsoft the benefit of the doubt, it could be the case that the update
revealed some bug in the audio drivers used on my system. Everything had been
working fine previously, though. I tried updating my audio drivers after the
problem first started (hence the recent driver date in the screenshot above),
but the problem is still happening.

As yet, there’s nothing related to this in the
[Windows 11 25H2 known issues list](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-gb/windows/release-health/status-windows-11-25h2#known-issues),
so I imagine that I’m going to have to put up with this for a bit longer[^2]
(that is, until someone takes responsibility for the bug).

[^1]: I say ‘my PC’, as I don’t know if the same steps will work for everyone.

[^2]:
    Admittedly, it seems to be happening less frequently in the last few days,
    but it’s still happening.
